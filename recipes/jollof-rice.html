Jollof Rice

Description

This Jollof rice recipe features a rich, flavorful rice dish popular in West Africa. It's cooked with tomatoes, onions, and spices, making it a perfect side or main dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 bell peppers, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped or 1 can of crushed tomatoes
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon white pepper
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup frozen peas (optional)
  • 1 cup sliced carrots (optional)
  • 1 cup cooked chicken or beef (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook until translucent.
  4. Stir in the bell peppers, tomatoes, garlic, and ginger. Cook until the tomatoes break down and the mixture is well combined.
  5. Add paprika, dried thyme, curry powder, white pepper, and bay leaves. Stir to combine and cook for about 1-2 minutes.
  6. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and water. Season with salt and black pepper. Bring to a boil.
  7. Stir in the rinsed rice. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 20-30 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  8. If using peas, carrots, or cooked chicken/beef, gently stir them into the rice during the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  9. Once the rice is cooked, remove the pot from heat and let it sit covered for about 10 minutes. Fluff with a fork before serving.
